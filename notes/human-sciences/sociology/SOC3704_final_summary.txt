# Law Study Notes

## Chapter 1: Introduction to Legal Principles
- **Legal Frameworks**: Understand the basic structure of legal systems.
- **Statutory Law**: Codified laws enacted by legislative bodies.
- **Case Law**: Legal principles established through judicial decisions.
- **Common Law vs. Civil Law**: Distinctions between systems based on precedents and written codes.

## Chapter 2: Constitutional Foundations
- **Constitutional Law**: Governs the interpretation and implementation of the constitution.
- **Legislation**: Statutes created by elected representatives.
- **Judicial Review**: Power of courts to assess the constitutionality of legislative acts.
- **Landmark Cases**:
  - *Marbury v. Madison* (1803): Established judicial review.

## Chapter 3: Administrative Law
- **Admin Agencies**: Bodies created by legislative acts to enforce regulations.
- **Rulemaking**: Agencies create rules to implement policies.
- **Adjudication**: Agencies resolve disputes through a trial-like process.
- **Case Reference**: *Chevron U.S.A., Inc. v. Natural Resources Defense Council, Inc.* (1984): Established a precedent for judicial deference to agency interpretations.

## Chapter 4: Contracts and Obligations
- **Contract Definition**: A legally enforceable agreement between two or more parties.
- **Elements of a Valid Contract**:
  - Offer
  - Acceptance
  - Consideration
  - Capacity
  - Legality
- **Defenses Against Enforcement**:
  - Duress
  - Fraud
  - Mistake

## Chapter 5: Torts and Civil Liability
- **Tort Law**: Governs civil wrongs causing harm or loss.
- **Types of Torts**:
  - Intentional Torts: Deliberate actions causing harm.
  - Negligence: Failure to exercise reasonable care.
  - Strict Liability: Liability without fault.
- **Relevant Case**: *Palsgraf v. Long Island Railroad Co.* (1928): Established principles for duty and proximate cause.

## Chapter 6: Property Law
- **Types of Property**:
  - Real Property: Land and permanent attachments.
  - Personal Property: Movable items.
- **Ownership Rights**: Understanding title, rights, and responsibilities.
- **Transfer of Property**: Sales, gifts, and inheritance.

## Chapter 7: Criminal Law
- **Criminal Offenses**: Acts classified as crimes, including misdemeanors and felonies.
- **Elements of a Crime**:
  - Actus Reus: The act itself.
  - Mens Rea: The intent behind the act.
- **Defenses**: Insanity, self-defense, duress.
- **Notable Case**: *Miranda v. Arizona* (1966): Established Miranda rights for suspects.

## Chapter 8: Family Law
- **Marriage and Divorce**: Legal requirements and processes.
- **Child Custody**: Determining best interests of the child.
- **Adoption**: Legal process for establishing parent-child relationships.
  
## Chapter 9: International Law
- **Sources of International Law**:
  - Treaties
  - Customary Law
  - Judicial Decisions
- **Key Institutions**: United Nations, International Criminal Court.
- **Case Example**: *Nicaragua v. United States* (1986): Established principles of state sovereignty and non-intervention.

## Chapter 10: Legal Ethics
- **Professional Responsibility**: Codes governing legal practice.
- **Conflicts of Interest**: Understanding client representation ethics.
- **Disciplinary Actions**: Circumstances leading to sanctions.

## Conclusion
- Mastery of legal principles requires the understanding of statutes, case law, and ethical guidelines. Regular updates on landmark cases will help keep knowledge relevant in legal practice.
