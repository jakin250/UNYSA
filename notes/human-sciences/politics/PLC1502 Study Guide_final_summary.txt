### 1. Introduction to Legal Principles
- **Definition of Law**: A set of rules enforced through social institutions.
- **Purpose of Law**: Maintain order, resolve disputes, protect liberties, and rights.

### 2. Sources of Law
- **Statutory Law**: Laws enacted by legislative bodies (e.g., Parliament).
- **Common Law**: Law developed through court decisions (case law).
- **Administrative Law**: Rules and regulations created by governmental agencies.
- **International Law**: Rules governing relations between countries.

### 3. Legal Theories
- **Natural Law Theory**: Law should be based on morality and ethics.
- **Positive Law Theory**: Law is the command of the sovereign.
- **Legal Realism**: Law is shaped by social, economic, and historical contexts.
- **Critical Legal Studies**: Challenges traditional legal doctrines and emphasizes societal contexts.
- **Law and Economics**: Examines the economic impact of laws and legal decisions.

### 4. Judicial Process
- **Court Hierarchy**: Structure of courts, including trial courts, appellate courts, and supreme courts.
- **Judicial Review**: Courts' ability to assess the constitutionality of legislative acts.
- **Precedent (Stare Decisis)**: Courts are bound by previous rulings in similar cases.

### 5. Legislation
- **Creation of Legislation**: Involves drafting, debating, and enacting laws.
- **Types of Statutes**: Civil statutes, criminal statutes, regulatory statutes.

### 6. Case Law
- **Importance of Case Law**: Provides interpretations and applications of statutes.
- **Major Cases Influencing Law**:
  - **Marbury v. Madison (1803)**: Established judicial review.
  - **Brown v. Board of Education (1954)**: Declared racial segregation unconstitutional.
  - **Roe v. Wade (1973)**: Recognized a woman's right to privacy regarding abortion.

### 7. Conclusion
- **Integration of Law**: Observing the interplay between legal principles, sources, and theories essential for comprehensive legal understanding.
- **Continued Legal Development**: The law must evolve with societal changes, keeping pace with new challenges and technologies.

- Contract law governs the agreements made between two or more parties.
- Binding agreements are enforced by law.

### 1.2 Types of Contracts
- Valid Contracts: Legally enforceable agreements.
- Void Contracts: Agreements that lack legal effect.
- Voidable Contracts: Agreements that can be invalidated at the option of one party.

### 1.3 Elements of a Contract
- Offer: Proposal made by one party.
- Acceptance: Agreement to the offer by the other party.
- Consideration: Something of value exchanged.
- Intention: Parties must intend to create legal relations.
- Capacity: Parties must have the legal ability to contract.

### 1.4 Remedies for Breach of Contract
- Damages: Monetary compensation for loss.
- Specific Performance: Court order to fulfill contractual obligations.
- Rescission: Cancelation of the contract.

# Legal Study Notes

## Chapter 1: Introduction to Legal Principles

### 1.1 Overview of Law
- Law is a system of rules recognized by a country or community.
- Governs the actions of its members.

### 1.2 Sources of Law
- **Legislation**: Statutes enacted by legislative bodies.
- **Common Law**: Laws developed through court decisions.
- **Customs and Conventions**: Practices accepted as legal norms.

### 1.3 Key Legal Concepts
- **Jurisdiction**: The authority of a court to hear a case.
- **Precedent**: Previous court decisions that influence future cases.
- **Ratio Decidendi**: The legal reason for a court decision.

## Chapter 2: Constitutional Law

### 2.1 Definition and Importance
- Constitutional law governs the structure and operation of government institutions.
- Ensures the protection of individual rights.

### 2.2 Key Articles and Amendments
- Emphasis on rights such as freedom of speech, due process, and equal protection.

## Chapter 3: Contract Law

### 3.1 Nature of Contracts
- Contracts are legally binding agreements between parties.
- Essential elements: Offer, acceptance, intent, and consideration.

### 3.2 Types of Contracts
- **Bilateral vs. Unilateral**: Promises exchanged vs. one-sided promise.
- **Express vs. Implied**: Written/spoken vs. inferred from actions.

### 3.3 Breach of Contract
- Occurs when a party fails to fulfill contractual obligations.
- Remedies: Damages, specific performance, or rescission.

## Chapter 4: Tort Law

### 4.1 Definition of Torts
- A tort is a civil wrongdoing that causes harm or loss.
- Aims to provide relief to individuals harmed by the actions of others.

### 4.2 Types of Torts
- **Negligence**: Failure to take reasonable care.
- **Intentional Torts**: Deliberate actions causing harm.
- **Strict Liability**: Liability without fault or negligence.

### 4.3 Defenses to Tort Claims
- **Consent**: Agreement to the conduct.
- **Comparative Negligence**: Damages reduced based on the plaintiff's contribution.

## Chapter 5: Criminal Law

### 5.1 Nature of Criminal Law
- Governs actions that are considered offenses against the state.
- Aims to deter criminal conduct and punish offenders.

### 5.2 Elements of a Crime

## Chapter 7: Family Law
