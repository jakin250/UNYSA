## Chapter 1: Legal Principles Overview

### Learning Unit 1.1: Fundamental Legal Concepts
- **Law Definition**: System of rules recognized by a country/community as regulating the actions of its members.
- **Legal Systems**: Common law vs. civil law; distinguishes judicial interpretations and statutory laws.
- **Sources of Law**: Legislation, case law, custom, and conventions. 

### Learning Unit 1.2: Principles of Justice
- **Equality Before the Law**: All individuals treated equally.
- **Rule of Law**: No one is above the law; law applies equally.
- **Access to Justice**: Right to seek judicial remedies.

---

## Chapter 2: Statutory Interpretation

### Learning Unit 2.1: Methods of Interpretation
- **Literal Rule**: Words interpreted in their plain, ordinary meaning.
- **Golden Rule**: Modifies literal interpretation to avoid absurdity.
- **Mischief Rule**: Focuses on the law's purpose and issues remedied.

### Learning Unit 2.2: Relevant Legislation
- **Statute Law**: Acts of Parliament that are required to be followed.
- **Interpretation Acts**: Provides guidelines on how statutes should be understood.

---

## Chapter 3: Case Law Analysis

### Learning Unit 3.1: The Importance of Precedent
- **Stare Decisis**: Principle that binds courts to follow precedent established by higher courts.
- **Binding vs Persuasive Precedents**: Binding from higher courts within the same jurisdiction; persuasive from other jurisdictions.

### Learning Unit 3.2: Leading Cases
- **Key Case Law**: Landmark decisions that shaped legal principles;
  - **Case X v. Y**: Set precedent for negligence.
  - **Case A v. B**: Established principles of contract law.

### Learning Unit 3.3: Judicial Reasoning
- **Ratio Decidendi**: The reason for the decision; forms binding precedent.
- **Obiter Dicta**: Remarks made by a judge that are not necessary for the decision; persuasive, not binding.

## Chapter 1: Introduction to Law
- Overview of Law: 
  - Definition and functions of law
  - Sources of law: statutes, common law, regulations
  
### 1.1 Principles of Law
- Rule of Law: principle that all individuals and institutions are accountable to the law.
- Separation of Powers: distribution of government powers among different branches.

## Chapter 2: Legal Systems
### 2.1 Types of Legal Systems
- Common Law: legal system based on judicial decisions and precedents.
- Civil Law: legal system based on written codes and statutes.

## Chapter 3: Contract Law
### 3.1 Essentials of a Contract
- Offer: a promise or proposal.
- Acceptance: agreement to the terms of the offer.
- Consideration: something of value exchanged.

### 3.2 Types of Contracts
- Bilateral: mutual promises made by both parties.
- Unilateral: one party makes a promise in exchange for an act.

### 3.3 Legislation and Cases
- **Statute of Frauds**: requires certain contracts to be in writing.
- **Carlill v. Carbolic Smoke Ball Co** [1893]: established the importance of offer and acceptance.

## Chapter 4: Tort Law
### 4.1 Foundations of Tort Law
- Definition: a civil wrong causing harm or loss.
- Types of Torts:
  - Intentional: deliberate actions leading to harm.
  - Negligence: failure to act with reasonable care.
  
### 4.2 Key Cases
- **Donoghue v. Stevenson** [1932]: established duty of care.
- **Bolam v. Friern Hospital Management Committee** [1957]: standard of care in medical negligence.

## Chapter 5: Criminal Law
### 5.1 Principles of Criminal Law
- Definition: body of law concerning crimes and their prosecution.
- Key Components:
  - Actus Reus: the guilty act.
  - Mens Rea: the guilty mind.

### 5.2 Defenses in Criminal Law
- Insanity: inability to understand the nature of the act.
- Self-defense: justifiable use of force in protection.

## Chapter 6: Constitutional Law
### 6.1 Structure of Government
- Three Branches: Executive, Legislative, Judicial.
  
### 6.2 Fundamental Rights
- Right to free speech, right to fair trial, and right to privacy.

## Chapter 7: Evidence Law
### 7.1 Types of Evidence
- Documentary: written evidence.
- Testimonial: oral evidence from witnesses.

### 7.2 Admissibility of Evidence
- Rules governing what can be used in court.

## Chapter 8: International Law
### 8.1 Definition and Sources
- Governs the legal responsibilities of states and international organizations.
- Sources: treaties, custom, general principles of law.

### 8.2 Key Conventions
- **Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties**: governs treaty-making.

## Chapter 9: Dispute Resolution
### 9.1 Methods of Dispute Resolution
- Litigation: legal proceedings in court.
- Arbitration: binding resolution by a third-party arbitrator.
- Mediation: facilitative approach to resolve disputes.

### 9.2 Key Legislation
- **Arbitration Act 1996**: frameworks for arbitration in the UK.
