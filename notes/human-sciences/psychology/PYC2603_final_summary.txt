### Page 5-4: Statutory Interpretation

- **Literal Rule**: Interpret words in their plain meaning.
- **Golden Rule**: Modify the literal meaning to avoid absurdity.
- **Mischief Rule**: Consider the law's purpose and try to suppress the mischief the act was intended to remedy.

### Page 5-5: Key Legislation and Cases

- **Key Legislation**:
  - *Civil Rights Act*: Address discrimination.
  - *Contract Law Statutes*: Govern contractual obligations.

- **Notable Cases**:
  - *Brown v. Board of Education*: Landmark decision in civil rights.
  - *Marbury v. Madison*: Established judicial review.

### Page 5-6: Legal Theories

- **Natural Law**: Posits that laws are based on inherent moral principles.
- **Legal Positivism**: Asserts that laws are valid when enacted by legitimate authority, regardless of their moral content.
- **Critical Legal Studies**: Challenges traditional legal doctrines; considers social justice.

## Chapter Y: Contract Law

### Learning Unit 1: Elements of a Contract
- **Offer**: A proposal to enter into a legal agreement.
- **Acceptance**: An agreement to the terms of the offer.
- **Consideration**: Something of value exchanged (e.g., money, services).

### Learning Unit 2: Types of Contracts
- **Bilateral Contracts**: Mutual exchange of promises.
- **Unilateral Contracts**: One party fulfills a promise upon completion of an act.
- **Express Contracts**: Terms explicitly stated.
- **Implied Contracts**: Terms inferred by actions.

### Learning Unit 3: Breach of Contract
- **Types of Breaches**:
  - Minor Breach: Partial failure to fulfill contract.
  - Material Breach: Significant failure allowing for legal remedy.
- **Remedies**:
  - Compensatory Damages: Monetary compensation.
  - Specific Performance: Court order to fulfill contractual obligations.

## Chapter Z: Criminal Law

### Learning Unit 1: Elements of a Crime
- **Actus Reus**: The act or conduct that constitutes a criminal offense.
- **Mens Rea**: The mental state or intent to commit a crime.

### Learning Unit 2: Types of Crimes
- **Felonies**: Serious crimes (e.g., murder, robbery).
- **Misdemeanors**: Lesser offenses (e.g., theft, disorderly conduct).
- **Infractions**: Minor violations (e.g., traffic offenses).

### Learning Unit 3: Defenses in Criminal Law
- **Insanity Defense**: Lack of mental capacity.
- **Self-Defense**: Justification for use of force to protect oneself.
- **Duress**: Coercion that negates liability.

## Chapter A: Tort Law

### Learning Unit 1: Elements of Tort
- **Duty of Care**: Obligation to avoid causing harm.
- **Breach of Duty**: Failure to meet the standard of care.
- **Causation**: Direct link between breach and harm caused.

### Learning Unit 2: Types of Torts
- **Intentional Torts**: Deliberate actions causing harm (e.g., assault, battery).
- **Negligence**: Unintentional harm due to carelessness.
- **Strict Liability**: Liability without fault for defective products.

### Learning Unit 3: Remedies for Torts
- **Compensatory Damages**: Compensation for loss or injury.
- **Punitive Damages**: Additional damages to deter wrongful conduct.
