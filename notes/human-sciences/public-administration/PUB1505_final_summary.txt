# Study Notes: Public Supply Chain Management

## 1.1 Obligations under the Constitution
- **Section 217(3)**: Mandates national legislation for preferential procurement to remedy past socio-economic disparities.
- **Sections 215–219**: Require the National Treasury to establish norms for transparency and expenditure control in government spheres.
- **Section 195(1)**: Public administration must adhere to democratic values:
  - Professional ethics
  - Efficient resource use
  - Development-oriented administration
  - Fair and impartial service provisioning
  - Public responsive policymaking
  - Accountability
  - Transparency of information

## 1.2 Regulatory Framework for SCM 
### 1.2.1 Framework Established by PFMA
- **PFMA (1999; Act 1 of 1999, amended by Act 29 of 1999)**: 
  - Governs financial management in national/provincial governments.
  - Emphasizes: Fairness, equity, transparency, competition, and cost-effectiveness in procurement systems.
- **Framework for Supply Chain Management (effective 5 December 2003)**:
  - Approved by the Minister of Finance, guides supply chain management in national and provincial departments.

### 1.2.2 State Tender Board Act Amendment
- Amendments (Government Gazette): Allow accounting officers to procure either through the State Tender Board or PFMA.
- Promotes accountability and responsibility for departmental expenditures.

### 1.3 Legislation Impacting SCM 
#### 1.3.1 Objectives of Legislation
- Addresses societal problems.
- Provides statutory guidelines for achieving objectives.
- Grants authority for policy execution.

#### 1.3.2 Public Finance Management Act (PFMA)
- **Objectives**: Regulate financial management, ensure efficient management of public resources.
- **Sections 38(1)(a)(i) & 76(4)(c)**: Stipulate requirements for procurement regulations under National Treasury.

#### 1.3.3 Guide for Accounting Officers
- Aims to facilitate integrated SCM understanding and implementation across government.

#### 1.3.4 Municipal Finance Management Act (MFMA, 2003)
- Aims to modernize municipal financial management.
- Establishes SCM as essential to municipal governance, impacting service delivery.
- **Section 62(1)(f)(iv)**: Mandates municipal managers to implement fair, equitable, and transparent SCM policy.

#### 1.3.5 Preferential Procurement Policy Framework Act (PPPF, Act 5 of 2000)
- Addresses procurement as a tool for rectifying past injustices.
- **Objectives**:
  - Alleviate historical inequalities.
  - Enhance flexibility in procurement.
  - Combat fronting and corruption.

#### 1.3.6 Preferential Procurement Regulations (August 2001)
- Highlight key procurement processes:
  - Require careful planning prior to tender invitations.
  - Establish penalties for non-compliance or misrepresentation.
  - Dictate the award process based on scoring systems.
  - Include provisions for HDI ownership in evaluations.

## 1.4 Accountability through Integrated Procurement Systems
- **Definition**: Holding individuals/institutions accountable for actions and performance.
- Emphasized through PFMA, requiring individual accountability for decisions.
- **Accountability Actions**:
  - Annual Reports and Auditor-General's assessments highlight adherence to strategic objectives and areas needing improvement.

### 1.5 SUPPLY CHAIN MANAGEMENT
- Accountability chain enhances public sector financial management.
- PFMA empowers accounting officers to manage financial affairs.
- Introduction of supply chain management (SCM) concept by Cabinet.

#### Four Major Objectives of SCM Policy:
- Transform procurement into an integrated SCM function.
- Systematic approach for consultant appointments.
- Common understanding of preferential procurement policy.
- Consistent application of best practices across government.

### 1.5.1 Concepts of Supply Chain Management
- SCM involves planning, design, control of information and materials.
- It aims to meet current and future customer needs efficiently.
- Includes managing working capital related to goods and services.
- Applicable to all public entities, constitutional institutions, and municipalities.

- **SCM Legislation**: 
  - Relevant for constitutional institutions, public entities, national/provincial departments, municipalities, and school governing bodies.

- **International Best Practices**:
  - Integrated SCM to add value at each process stage.
  - Focus on procurement, contract management, and asset disposal.

- **Office of the Chief Procurement Officer (OCPO)**: 
  - Established in 2013 to monitor procurement.
  - Responsibilities include modernizing SCM, ensuring transparency and fairness, developing SCM policies, and providing training.

### 1.5.2 Elements of Supply Chain Management

#### 1.5.2.1 Demand Management
- Initiates with needs assessment to satisfy institutional requirements.
- Must align with strategic and operational commitments.
- **Regulatory Framework** (Effective from 5 Dec 2003): 
  - Ensures understanding of needs, links to budget, determines specifications, and assesses past expenditures.

#### 1.5.2.2 Acquisition Management
- Focuses on the procurement process.
- Addresses management considerations like:
  - Identification of preferential policy objectives.
  - Market approach strategy.
  - Total cost of ownership (TCO) principle.
  - Contract administration and documentation.

#### 1.5.2.3 Logistics Management
- Concerned with the management of goods and related processes.
- Involves inventory management, transport management, contract administration, and vendor performance evaluation.

#### 1.5.2.4 Disposal Management
- Final phase addressing removal of unserviceable or redundant assets.
- Involves obsolescence planning and disposal strategy execution.

#### 1.5.2.5 Risk Management
- Must identify and manage potential risks in SCM.
- Includes allocation of risks, cost acceptance, and proactive management.

#### 1.5.2.6 Supply Chain Performance
- Retrospective analysis ensuring compliance with norms and cost efficiency.
- **Section 38(1)(a) PFMA**:
  - Stipulates maintenance of effective financial management systems, internal audit controls, and procurement systems.

## 2.3 Principles in Section 217(1) of the Constitution

- **Fair and Equitable**
  - Bids evaluated solely on advertised specifications.
  - Equal bidding opportunity for all suppliers.
  - Public advertisement in Government Tender Bulletin.
  - Timely consideration of all bids received.
  - No preferential treatment; bidders cannot amend offers unduly.
  - Unsuccessful bidders entitled to reasons for non-acceptance.

- **Transparent**
  - Bids and specifications clearly communicated to suppliers.
  - Public bid opening of timely submissions.
  - Disclosure of successful bidder information and rationale to unsuccessful bidders.

- **Competitive/Cost-effective**
  - Aim for lowest price with maximum competition for taxpayer benefits.
  - Accessibility to procurement opportunities is critical.
  - Costs of bidding should not deter capable suppliers.
  - Costs incurred for promoting competition must align with benefits received.

---

## 2.4 Equity

- **Equity Definition**
  - Refers to the application of government policies promoting advancement of disadvantaged individuals/groups.
  
- **Key Legislative Framework**
  - **PPPFA** (Preferential Procurement Policy Framework Act)
    - Aims to enhance opportunities for SMMEs, HDIs, and promote diversity and local enterprises.

---

## 2.5 Choice of Procurement Method

- **General Guidelines**
  - Procurements categorized based on monetary thresholds: petty cash, written/verbal quotations, formal written price quotations, competitive bidding.

- **Petty Cash Purchases**
  - For incidental expenses.
  - No competitive bids required; internal financial management procedures necessary.

- **Written or Verbal Quotations**
  - Applicable for amounts R2,000 to R10,000.
  - Minimum of three verbal quotes required; written confirmation necessary for orders.

- **Formal Written Price Quotations**
  - For estimates over R10,000 up to R500,000.
  - Must adhere to PPPFA principles for amounts above R30,000.

- **List of Prospective Suppliers**
  - Established per commodity for contracts under R500,000.
  - Regular updates to encourage inclusion of newly empowered businesses.
  - Requires rotation basis for inviting quotations to maintain competitiveness.

- **Central Supplier Database (CSD)**
  - Standardized database for supplier information; verification aligned with PFMA, MFMA.
  - Information includes business registration, tax compliance, and B-BBEE status.

---

## 2.5.4 Competitive Bidding

- **Application**
  - Required for all procurement above R500,000.
  - Advertisement in Government Tender Bulletin and potentially additional media.
  - Ensures timely notification and equal bidding opportunity aligned with cost-effectiveness.

### 2.6 Methods of Procurement

#### 2.6.1 Two-stage bidding
- Suitable for complex projects, projects without detailed specifications, long-term projects (>3 years).
- Applicable for tech-sensitive equipment (e.g., computers, communications).
- Allows bids for different approaches to fulfilling needs.
- Necessary to adhere to **PPPFA** regulations:
  - Points split for price and functionality allowed.
  - Points for HDI involvement and RDP goals required.
  - Evaluation methods must be documented in bid documents.

#### 2.6.2 Pre-qualifying bidders
- Pre-qualification involves criteria that bidders must meet prior to bidding.
- Commonly used for large or costly projects (e.g., custom equipment, industrial plants).
- Evaluation based on:
  - Capabilities (personnel, equipment, facilities).
  - Financial position.
- Successful pre-qualified bidders invited to submit specific bids.
  
#### 2.6.3 Single source bidding
- Only one among limited bidders selected post-transparent pre-selection.
- Suitable for:
  - Creative works, patent rights, ongoing projects.
  - Urgent circumstances requiring rapid selection.
- Negative impacts include:
  - Lack of market information and competition.
  - Potential bias in awarding contracts.

#### 2.6.4 Sole provider
- Applicable when one bidder exists, often with patent/distribution rights.
- Price should not exceed prevailing retail prices.
- Bid documents should require:
  - Wholesale price disclosures.
  - Breakdown of cost elements (direct labor/materials, variable/fixed costs, profit).

#### 2.6.5 Public Private Partnerships (PPP)
- Facilitates cost-sharing for government projects.
- Compliance with **Treasury Regulation 16** necessary.

#### 2.6.6 Negotiations
- Engaged under specific conditions with approval:
  - Urgent needs where bidding is impractical.
  - Catastrophic events requiring immediate procurement.
  - Preferred bidders identified through competitive bidding.

#### 2.6.7 Establishment of a list of preferred suppliers
- Created via competitive bidding for recurring needs.
- Accelerates service delivery through approved lists.
- Must be advertised and evaluated by a committee.

#### 2.6.8 E-Procurement
- Online sourcing of goods/services encompassing full transaction process.
- Enhances efficiency in high-volume generic purchases (e.g., office supplies).

#### 2.6.9 Procurement from abroad
- Follow local procurement processes with added considerations:
  - Transport costs, lead times, administrative complexity.
  - Exchange rate/economic factors.

#### 2.6.10 Specific cases
- Non-competitive bids possible under certain justifications.
  
##### 2.6.10.1 Urgent and emergency cases
- Bids can be omitted if urgency necessitates.
- Record reasons and obtain approval for exceptions.

##### 2.6.10.2 Unsolicited bids
- Consideration possible if:
  - Unique, beneficial offering.
  - Sole provider.
  - Need established in strategic planning.

#### 2.6.11 Appointment of consultants
- Should utilize competitive bidding like other services.
- Governed by specific procurement guidelines.

#### 2.6.12 Transversal contracts
- Contracts required by multiple institutions to achieve economies of scale.
- Participation limits contracting for the same goods/services during contract tenure.
- Evaluated by joint committees involving multiple representatives.

### 2.7 Conclusion
- Cost-effectiveness in procurement means comparing price and quality to select the best value contractor.
- All public procurement must comply with constitutional principles of fairness, equity, transparency, competition, and cost-effectiveness.

# Study Notes on Public Procurement Processes

## 3.1 Introduction
- Importance of trained officials in bid processes.
- Ensuring public perception of fairness and transparency.

## 3.2 Guidelines in the Preparation Process
- Assess availability of items on existing contracts.
- Identify quantity and proper procurement method (e.g., price quotation, competitive bidding).
- Conduct planning and cost calculations before bid invitation.
- Determine applicable preference point system under **Preferential Procurement Policy Framework Act (PPPFA)**.

## 3.3 General and Special Conditions of Contract
- **General Conditions of Contract (GCC)** and **Special Conditions of Contract (SCC)** are part of bid documentation.
- Acceptance of these conditions is mandatory for bidders.
- GCC issued by National Treasury requires uniformity in bidding.
- Conflicts with SCC lead to SCC provisions prevailing.

### 3.3.1 General Conditions of Contract (GCC)
- **GCC Requirements**:
  - Section 15: Supplier warranties regarding goods (new, defect-free).
  - Section 20: Suppliers must inform about subcontracts; remain responsible for contract execution.
  - Section 9.1: Suppliers must ensure damage-proof packaging.
  - Section 16: Payment must be made within 30 days of invoice submission.

## 3.4 Prohibition of Set Asides and Use of Cost Estimates as Benchmarks

# Study Notes on Just Administrative Action

## 4.3 Just Administrative Action
- **Constitutional Basis**: 
  - **Section 33(1)**: Right to lawful, reasonable, and procedurally fair administrative action.
  - **Section 33(2)**: Right to written reasons upon adverse administrative action.
- **Importance in Procurement**: 
  - Bid adjudication committee members must be knowledgeable about SCM legislation and policies to ensure lawful, reasonable, and fair treatment of bids.

## 4.4 Mandate, Role, and Function of the Bid Adjudication Committee
- **Treasury Regulation 16A6.2**: SCM systems must include a bid adjudication committee to prevent isolated decision-making by any one individual.
- **Committee Role**: Not limited to evaluating recommendations; may also review specifications from the bid specification committee to ensure:
  - Unbiased specifications and proper Terms of Reference.
  - Application of strategic sourcing principles and availability of funds.
  - Inclusion of appropriate special requirements.
  - Compliance with Preferential Procurement Regulations.

### 4.4.1 Composition of Bid Adjudication Committees and Appointment of Members
- **Appointment**: Written appointment by the accounting officer; must include at least four senior officials, with at least one supply chain practitioner.
- **Chairperson**: Preferably the Chief Financial Officer (CFO).
- **Quorum**: At least 60% presence required for decision-making.
- **Confidentiality Obligations**: Members must declare financial interests and confidentiality at each meeting.

### 4.4.2 Bid Adjudication Meetings and Procedures
#### 4.4.2.1 Notice of Meetings
- Agenda must be circulated at least three working days before the meeting.

#### 4.4.2.2 Submissions/Reports to the Committee
- All reports must be in writing and substantiated through prior committees.

#### 4.4.2.3 Minutes
- Written records reflecting decisions; dissenting opinions must be recorded.
- Minutes serve as proof of committee actions.

#### 4.4.2.4 Register
- Attendance and confidentiality undertaking must be signed by members.
- Declarations of gifts/invitations from suppliers must be recorded.

## 4.5 Award of Contract
- **Contract Validity**: Must award while bids are still valid (usually 60 days from tender closure).
- **Contract Formation**: Concluded upon unconditional acceptance of a bid during validity period.
- **Acceptance Communication**: Must be posted via registered mail, date-stamped by SAPO as proof of timely acceptance.

## 4.6 Contract Management
- **Public Interest**: State's duty to procure supplies/services under the best conditions while adhering to contractual terms.
- **Consensus Required**: Both parties must agree on terms before conclusion; obligations must be fulfilled.

### 4.6.1 Safekeeping of Contracts
- Institutions must secure original contract documents in a monitored environment.
- Original documentation essential for legal recourse during contract execution.

## 4.7 Risk Management and Contract Administration
- **Risk Allocation**: Clear documentation aids in assigning responsibilities.
  
### 4.7.1 Performance Security
- **GCC Paragraph 7**: Successful bidders must provide performance security by a specified amount as a safeguard against default.

### 4.7.2 Termination for Default
- Termination procedures under **section 23 of the GCC** allow for procurement of similar goods if a contract is partially or wholly terminated due to supplier default.

## 4.8 Fraud and Corruption
- **Ethics Requirement**: Government mandates high standards in contract execution and selection.
- **Definitions**:
  - **Corrupt Practice**: Influencing public officials through value offerings.
  - **Fraudulent Practice**: Misrepresentation of facts affecting contract processes.

- **Proposal Rejection**: Institutions must reject bids if corruption or fraud in bidding is discovered.
- **Prohibited Practices**: Include fictitious bids and creation of non-existent competition.

# Study Notes on Appointment of Consultants

### 5.1 INTRODUCTION
- Procedures for selecting, contracting, and monitoring consultants for projects.
- Focus on intellectual and advisory services.
- Excludes general services: construction, goods manufacture, facility operation, etc.

### 5.2 DEFINITION OF THE TERM CONSULTANT
- Professional providing advice or services in a specific field.
- Typically self-employed or part of a consultancy firm.
- Relationship: purchaser/provider, regulated by a contract.
- Pertinent legislation: **Public Service Act, 1994 (PSA)**.

### 5.3 NEED FOR APPOINTMENT
- Engage consultants when internal skills/resources are lacking or recruitment/training isn't feasible.
- Departments must have policies ensuring:
  - High-quality services.
  - Economy and efficiency.
  - Opportunities for qualified consultants.
  - Transparency in selection.
- Committees ensure:
  - Proper qualification of selected consultants.
  - Adherence to project schedules.
  - Alignment of services with project needs.

### 5.4 PROMOTING GOVERNMENT’S PREFERENTIAL POLICIES
- Appointment subject to **Preferential Procurement Regulations, 2000**.
- Involvement of Historically Disadvantaged Individuals (HDIs) and RDP goals is mandatory.

### 5.5 STEPS TO FOLLOW WHEN SELECTING CONSULTANTS
- Four distinct stages recommended for selection.
- Advertising and proposal opening follows standard processes for acquisitions.

### 5.6 IDENTIFY THE APPROACH
- Commonly recommended: **Quality and Cost-Based Selection (QCBS)**.
- Other approaches include:
  - Quality-based selection.
  - Selection under a fixed budget.
  - Least cost selection.
  - Single source selection.
- Selection approach determined by:
  - Assignment scope.
  - Service quality.
  - Assignment complexity.

- Decision questions for selecting appropriate consultant:
  - Is the assignment complex or specialised? 
    - If not, use QCBS.
    - If yes, consider **Quality-Based Selection (QBS)** for innovation. 

### Relevant Legislation
- **Public Service Act, 1994**.
- **Preferential Procurement Regulations, 2000**. 

### Case References
- Not specified; however, attention should be on compliance and effectiveness in procurement practices in line with aforementioned legal frameworks.

### 5.7 SELECTION OF PARTICULAR TYPES OF CONSULTANTS

- **5.7.1 Non-governmental Organisations (NGOs)**
  - Unique qualifications due to local knowledge.
  - May use **QCBS** for evaluation, focusing on criteria like:
    - Voluntarism
    - Non-profit status
    - Local expertise
  - Possible **single-source selection** if criteria are met.

- **5.7.2 Inspection Agents**
  - Employed for inspecting and certifying goods (quality, quantity, price).
  - Must be registered with **SANAS**; use competitive bidding for selection.

- **5.7.3 Banks**
  - Engaged for asset sales and financial transactions.
  - Selected under **QCBS**; RFP must detail selection criteria, including experience.
  - Compensation includes a “success fee” linked to asset value.

- **5.7.4 Auditors**
  - Selected via **QCBS** or **Least-Cost Selection** under defined TOR.
  - Tariffs set by **Auditor-General** and **SAICA** serve as benchmarks.

- **5.7.5 Service Delivery Contractors**
  - Involves hiring for service delivery (e.g., social workers).
  - Project documentation to detail job descriptions and selection procedures.

---

### 5.8 REQUESTS FOR BIDS

- **5.8.1 Terms of Reference (TOR)**
  - Departments must draft their own TOR.
  - Scope of services aligned with budget.
  - Clearly outlines task directives, objectives, background info, and required outputs.
  - To include:
    - Training objectives with specifics.
    - Evaluation criteria correlated with TOR.
    - Cost estimates based on staffing/logistics.
    - Composition of evaluation panel.
    - Experience requirement disclosures for consultants.

- **5.8.2 Request for Proposals (RFP)**
  - Utilized for projects needing both quality and cost evaluation.
  - Should include:
    - **Letter of Invitation (LOI)**: Contract intention details.
    - **Information to Consultants (ITC)**: Evaluation process transparency.
    - **Terms of Reference (TOR)**: Defines project scope and methodology.
    - **Contract**: Use **Standard Form of Contract** issued by **NT**.

---

### 5.9 RECEIPT OF PROPOSALS

- Allow 4 weeks to 3 months for proposal preparation.
- Clarifications available in writing to all bidders.
- Submit technical and financial proposals simultaneously in sealed envelopes.
- No post-deadline modifications accepted; late proposals returned unopened.

---

### 5.10 EVALUATION OF BIDS/PROPOSALS

- Bids evaluated per **Preferential Procurement Regulations, 2001**.
- Criteria: functionality, price, achievement of specified RDP goals.

### 5.11 Negotiations and Awarding of Contract 
- Evaluation in two phases: functionality (quality) followed by price.
- Total percentage allocation for both must equal 100%; division ratio approved by accounting officer.
- Panel members use prepared score sheets containing criteria and weights from TOR.
- Members must evaluate independently without discussions.
- No additional criteria permitted post-bid closure.
- Score sheets signed by panel members; written motivations required for significant discrepancies.

#### 5.11.1 General Conditions of Contract
- **5.11.1.1 Definitions**
  - *Closing time*: Date & hour for bid receipt.
  - *Contract*: Written agreement between purchaser and supplier.
  - *Contract price*: Price payable for proper contract performance.
  - *Corrupt practice*: Influencing public officials in procurement.
  - *Countervailing duties*: Measures against foreign subsidies.
  - *Country of origin*: Where goods/services are produced.
  - *Delivery*: Compliance with contract conditions; types defined (ex stock, into store).
  - *Dumping*: Selling goods in RSA at lower prices than in country of origin.
  - *Force majeure*: Events outside supplier's control.
  - *Fraudulent practice*: Misrepresentation by bidders, including collusion.
  - *GCC*: General Conditions of Contract.
  - *Goods*: Equipment/materials supplied under contract.
  - *Imported content*: Cost of components imported.
  - *Local content*: Non-imported portion of bidding price.
  - *Manufacture*: Production activities in a factory.
  - *Order*: Official written request for supply.
  - *Purchaser*: Entity buying goods/services.

- **5.11.1.2 Application**
  - General conditions apply to bids/contracts excluding immovable property unless stated otherwise. 

- **5.11.1.3 General**
  - Purchaser not liable for bid preparation expenses; potential non-refundable fees for documents.

- **5.11.1.4 Standards**
  - Goods must meet specified standards in bidding documents.

- **5.11.1.5 Use of Contract Documents**
  - Supplier must not disclose contract specifics without prior consent; documents remain purchaser property.

- **5.11.1.6 Patent Rights**
  - Supplier must indemnify purchaser against third-party claims of infringement.

- **5.11.1.7 Performance Security**
  - Successful bidder to provide security within 30 days; various acceptable forms specified.

- **5.11.1.8 Inspections, Tests, Analyses**
  - Pre-bidding testing at bidder's cost; compliance inspections by purchaser representatives; non-compliance leads to supplier costs.

- **5.11.1.9 Packing**
  - Supplier responsible for adequate packing to prevent damage; must comply with contract specifications.

- **5.11.1.10 Delivery and Documents**
  - Delivery per contract terms; specifics outlined in SCC.

- **5.11.1.11 Insurance**
  - Goods fully insured against loss or damage in specified currency.

- **5.11.1.12 Transportation**
  - Non-inclusive prices for transportation specified in SCC.

### 5.11.1.13 Incidental Services
- Supplier may provide additional services as specified in the SCC, including:
  - On-site assembly/commissioning supervision.
  - Furnishing tools for assembly/maintenance.
  - Detailed operations and maintenance manual.
  - Maintenance/repair services agreed upon, without relieving warranty obligations.
  - Training of purchaser’s personnel at supplier's plant/on-site.
- Prices for incidental services must be agreed in advance and not exceed prevailing rates for similar services.

### 5.11.1.14 Spare Parts
- Supplier may provide materials/notifications regarding spare parts as specified in SCC:
  - Spare parts purchaser might elect to buy.
  - Notification of termination of production:
    - Advance notification to purchaser.
    - Provision of blueprints/drawings post-termination at no cost if requested.

### 5.11.1.15 Warranty
- Supplier warrants goods are new, of the latest models, and conform to contract.
- Warranty for defects: Covers design, materials, or workmanship (exceptions apply).
- Warranty period: 12 months post-delivery or 18 months post-shipment, whichever is earlier.
- Purchaser must notify supplier promptly of warranty claims; supplier must repair/replace within specified SCC time.

### 5.11.1.16 Payment
- Payment conditions specified in SCC; invoices require delivery note copy.
- Payments due within 30 days of invoice submission, generally in South African rand.

### 5.11.1.17 Prices
- Prices for goods/services shall match quoted bid prices, except authorized adjustments in SCC.

### 5.11.1.18 Contract Amendments
- Variations to contract terms require written amendments signed by both parties.

### 5.11.1.19 Assignment
- Supplier cannot assign contractual obligations without prior written consent from purchaser.

### 5.11.1.20 Subcontracts
- Supplier must notify purchaser of all subcontracts; liability remains with supplier regardless of notification.

### 5.11.1.21 Delays in Performance
- Timely delivery as per purchaser schedule; supplier must notify of delays and their causes.
- Purchaser may extend delivery time or procure substitutes without contract cancellation; penalties may apply.

### 5.11.1.22 Penalties
- Failure to perform on time results in penalties; amounts deducted from contract price based on delays.

### 5.11.1.23 Termination for Default
- Purchaser can terminate in whole/part upon supplier default (failure to deliver, perform obligations, engagement in corrupt practices).
- Purchaser may procure similar goods/services from other sources, and excess costs are chargeable to the supplier.

### 5.11.1.24 Force Majeure
- Supplier not liable for damages due to force majeure; must notify purchaser and continue obligations as practicable.

### 5.11.1.25 Termination for Insolvency
- Purchaser may terminate contract if supplier becomes insolvent, without compensation.

### 5.11.1.26 Limitation of Liability
- Disputes to be resolved via consultation; unresolved disputes can proceed to mediation or South African court.
- Supplier's liability limited to total contract price, excluding issues by supplier's negligence or equipment repair.

### 5.11.1.27 Anti-Dumping and Countervailing Duties
- Supplier responsible for provisional payments or duties post-bid date; provisions for favorable differences outlined.

### 5.11.1.28 Governing Language
- Contracts must be in English; all correspondence shall also be in English.

### 5.11.1.29 Applicable Law
- Contract interpretation governed by South African laws, unless specified otherwise.

### 5.11.1.30 Notices
- Notices served by registered mail; dates for actions computed from notice posting date.

### 5.11.1.31 Taxes and Duties
- Foreign suppliers bear taxes outside purchaser's country; local suppliers responsible for taxes until delivery.
- Tax clearance certificate required before contract awarding; no contracts granted without it.

### 5.11.1.32 National Industrial Participation (NIP) Programme
- NIP Programme applies to applicable contracts as administered by the Department of Trade and Industry.

### 5.12 CONTRACT AWARD
- Contract awarded based on highest points under PPPFA unless justified; accounting officer must defend decisions deviating from points criteria.

## 5.13 TYPES OF CONTRACTS

- **Lump Sum (Firm Fixed Price) Contract**  
  - Used for clearly defined services and outputs.  
  - Payments linked to deliverables (reports, designs).

- **Time-Based Contract**  
  - Suitable for undefined scope/length services.  
  - Payments based on set rates (hourly, daily, etc.) plus reimbursables.  
  - Ceiling amount required, including contingencies.

- **Retainer and/or Contingency Fee Contract**  
  - Common in mergers or sales preparation.  
  - Combines fixed retainer and success fee (percentage of sale price).

- **Percentage Contract**  
  - Fees based on project cost, primarily for architectural services.  
  - Must be based on fixed target costs; discouraged for lack of economic incentive.

- **Indefinite Delivery Contract (Price Agreement)**  
  - "On call" services for undefined activities.  
  - Unit rates agreed upon; payments for actual time used.

## 5.14 EVALUATION OF THE PERFORMANCE OF CONSULTANTS

- *Due Diligence:* Consultants required to meet standards.  
- *Evaluation Process:* Fair and confidential; opportunity for explanation of performance issues.  
- *Modifications:* Significant changes require approval from accounting officer/authority.

## 5.15 DEBRIEFING

- *Request Procedure:* Written request to accounting officer for rationale on non-selection.  
- *Dissatisfaction Resolution:* Further escalation to treasury, public protector, or court.

## 5.16 CONCLUSION

- Importance of a systematic approach in selecting consultants.  
- Monitoring of consultant performance ensures informed future decisions.  
- Emphasis on professionalism and trust within supply chain management.
