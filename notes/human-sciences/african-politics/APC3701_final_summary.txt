  - Laws enacted by legislative bodies.
- Common Law
  - Law developed through court decisions; judicial precedents.
- Case Law
  - Interpretations of statutes and regulations in court cases.

- Rule of Law
  - No one is above the law; laws apply equally to all.
- Separation of Powers
  - Division of government responsibilities into distinct branches.
- Judicial Review
  - The power of courts to assess the constitutionality of legislative and executive actions.

## 4. Court Structure
- Trial Courts
  - First instance courts where cases are tried.
- Appellate Courts
  - Courts that review decisions from lower courts.
- Supreme Court
  - The highest court; final arbiter on matters of law.

## 5. Legal Procedures
- Civil Procedure
  - Processes for civil litigation including pleadings and trial.
- Criminal Procedure
  - Processes pertaining to criminal prosecutions.
- Alternative Dispute Resolution
  - Methods such as mediation and arbitration to resolve disputes outside of court.

  - Essential elements: Offer, acceptance, consideration, and intention.
- Enforceability
  - Valid contracts are enforceable in a court of law.
- Breach of Contract
  - Failure to fulfill contractual obligations.

## 7. Tort Law
- Negligence
  - Failure to exercise reasonable care leading to damage or injury.
- Intentional Torts
  - Deliberate actions causing harm to another (e.g., assault).
- Strict Liability
  - Responsibility without fault; applies to inherently dangerous activities.

## 8. Criminal Law
- Elements of Crimes
  - Actus Reus (guilty act) and Mens Rea (guilty mind).
- Defenses
  - Legal justifications for actions (e.g., self-defense).

## 9. Constitutional Law

# Law Study Notes

## Chapter 1: Legal Principles

### 1.1 Overview of Legal Framework
- Legal systems categorized into common law and civil law.
- Legislation is the primary source of law (statutes, regulations).
- Judicial decisions (precedents) shape interpretation of laws.

### 1.2 Key Legislation
- **Constitution**: Fundamental legal document outlining rights and government structure.
- **Statutory Law**: Laws enacted by legislative bodies.
- **Regulatory Law**: Rules made by governmental agencies to enforce statutory laws.

### 1.3 Case Law
- **Judicial Review**: Courts’ authority to invalidate laws/decisions against the Constitution.
- Landmark cases demonstrate principles of justice and legal interpretation.
    - **Marbury v. Madison**: Established the principle of judicial review.

## Chapter 2: Legal Concepts and Theories

### 2.1 Theories of Law
- **Natural Law**: Moral is inherent, and legal systems should reflect morality.
- **Legal Positivism**: Law is a set of rules and standards enforceable by the state.
- **Critical Legal Studies**: Challenges traditional laws, emphasizing social context and implications.

### 2.2 Important Legal Concepts
- **Rights**: Privileges or entitlements recognized by law (human rights, property rights).
- **Justice**: Fairness in protection of rights and punishment of wrongs.
- **Liability**: Legal responsibility for one’s actions or inactions.

### 2.3 Essential Case Principles
- **Negligence**: Standard of care owed by individuals.
- **Due Process**: Fair treatment through the judicial system.
- **Equal Protection**: Laws must treat individuals equally without discrimination.

## Chapter 3: Court Systems and Procedures

### 3.1 Overview of Court Structure
- **Federal Courts**: Handle cases involving federal law; includes Supreme Court.
- **State Courts**: Handle cases under state law, covering the majority of legal disputes.

### 3.2 Court Procedures
- **Litigation Process**: Steps include filing, discovery, trial, and appeals.
- **Civil vs. Criminal Procedure**: 
    - Civil: Disputes between individuals/groups.
    - Criminal: State vs. individual regarding penal offenses.

### 3.3 Important Procedures
- **Jurisdiction**: Court’s authority to hear a case, includes subject matter and personal jurisdiction.
- **Standing**: The required legal interest to bring a lawsuit.

## Chapter 4: Legal Ethics and Professional Responsibility

### 4.1 Legal Ethics
- Rules governing the conduct of lawyers (ABA Model Rules).
- Importance of maintaining integrity and confidentiality.

### 4.2 Professional Responsibility
- Duties owed to clients, the court, and the legal profession.
- Conflicts of interest must be disclosed and managed.

### 4.3 Disciplinary Measures
- Violations of legal ethics can lead to disciplinary action (reprimand, suspension, disbarment).

## Chapter 5: Understanding Legal Research

### 5.1 Legal Research Fundamentals
- Importance of primary and secondary sources.
- Research tools: legal databases (LexisNexis, Westlaw).

### 5.2 Stages of Legal Research
- Identifying legal issues, gathering relevant statutes/cases, analyzing legal precedents.
- Presenting findings in legal memoranda or briefs.

### 5.3 Writing Skills
- Clear and concise legal writing is essential.
- Structuring legal documents correctly according to the jurisdiction.

## Conclusion
- Legal principles, case law, and ethical standards form the basis of the legal system.
- Ongoing study and research are crucial for legal professionals.
